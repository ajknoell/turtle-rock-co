A note to founders

What this isn't — and what it is

Most of what lands in a business owner's inbox sounds the same. Someone promising "a seamless exit," throwing around multiples, and pushing you toward a decision you're not ready to make. That's not what I do.

I started my career in a technology startup, where I learned how businesses actually operate from the inside. I later moved into private equity, where I sat across from founders navigating some of the biggest decisions of their lives. The transitions that went well had one thing in common: the founder and the buyer saw eye-to-eye and took the time to build a real relationship before anything was signed. That's the standard I hold myself to.

I'm an individual, not a fund. My goal is to find one well-run business in a focused service vertical and operate it with the same care the original owner put in. Long-term stewardship, not a short-term hold.

I'm not here to sell it in three years or squeeze every dollar out of it before moving on. I want to run it, grow it, and take care of the people who depend on it.

Andrew Knoell Founder, Turtle Rock Co.
About Andrew

A background built for this

Andrew Knoell · Founder, Turtle Rock Co.

My career has lived at the intersection of technology, sales, and finance. These are areas where the difference between a good outcome and a bad one usually comes down to whether you understand people as well as you understand the numbers.

In sales, I learned how to listen before I speak and how to earn trust without rushing it. In tech, I learned how operations actually work: where the leverage is, where the friction hides, and how to make a team better without breaking what's already working.

In finance and deal work, I sat across from founders at pivotal moments. I watched them try to figure out who they could trust with what they'd built. I took notes on what they needed, not just in the transaction, but in the years that followed.

Now I'm on the other side of that table. Not as an advisor. As a buyer who intends to be the operator. Someone who will show up every day and take the business forward the way you'd want it taken.

What We Look For

Ten or more years in operation, with a track record that speaks for itself. Longevity signals reputation.

Owner-Operated

Built and run by the founder. No institutional capital, no PE backing. The kind of business where the owner's reputation is on the line every day.

Defensible revenue, recurring demand, and honest financials. We don't need explosive growth. We need something real and durable.

What to Expect

A process built around you

01

A straightforward conversation

The first step is getting to know each other. No pitch, no pressure. I want to understand your business, what you've built, and what matters to you about what comes next.

02

Mutual fit, mutual readiness

I'm actively looking to acquire, which means I need owners who are genuinely ready to have that conversation. I'm not here to pressure anyone, but I'm also not in a position to wait years. If the timing is wrong, I'll say so honestly.

03

Transparency throughout

No games, no bait and switch. You'll know exactly where we stand, what we can offer, and how a transition would work before you ever have to commit to anything.

04

A real transition plan, built together

Whether you stay involved for a year or hand over the keys on day one, we'll structure a transition that works for you, your team, and your customers.
